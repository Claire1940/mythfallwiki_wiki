<Section icon={Swords} title="Combat and Mechanics Blunders">

Mythfall features a high-quality, action-oriented combat system that demands active participation. Trying to play this like a traditional, passive Roblox simulator is one of the most punishing **mythfall mistakes** you can make.

### Ignoring the Parry and Dodge Mechanics
You cannot simply stand still and cycle through your hotbar abilities. Enemies, especially bosses, have heavy attacks that will quickly break your guard or deplete your health pool. 

* **The Blocking Limit**: Holding your block key absorbs damage, but it fills your block bar. Once full, your guard breaks, leaving you briefly stunned and completely vulnerable.
* **Unparriable Attacks**: Certain heavy red-telegraphed attacks cannot be parried. You must use your dodge roll to weave out of harm's way.
* **The Stagger System**: Bosses have a stagger bar. Depleting this bar stuns the boss, opening a critical window where they take increased damage. Time your high-damage skills for these moments.

<Section icon={Target} title="Resource and Build Mismanagement">

With nine unique weapon types, powerful magic paths, and five distinct races, character customization is incredibly deep. However, trying to master everything at once will severely dilute your character's power.

### Spreading Upgrades Too Thin
A highly common progression trap is leveling up multiple weapons simultaneously. Materials for blacksmithing and gear upgrades are scarce in the early game. If you split your resources across swords, daggers, and great axes, you will lack the raw damage required to clear Act 1 bosses.

### Copying End-Game Tier Lists Too Early
Chasing a "meta" build from a high-level community video often backfires. Many end-game builds rely on specific high-tier armors, late-game dungeon weapons, or maxed-out magic masteries that are completely inaccessible to beginners. 

| Upgrade System | Common Mistakes | Recommended Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Weapons** | Upgrading 3+ weapons at the same time | Choose one main weapon and commit to it |
| **Magic** | Picking elements based solely on raw DPS | Balance damage with utility or movement |
| **Crafting** | Selling raw ores and metals for quick gold | Save all mining resources for blacksmithing |
| **Rerolls** | Spending rare currencies on early-game stats | Hold rerolls until you obtain end-game gear |

<Section icon={Map} title="Progression and Exploration Errors">

The island-based open world of Mythfall is designed to be explored systematically. Straying too far from the intended path is a quick way to stall your leveling progress.

### Skipping Story Quests
Attempting to level up solely by farming random island mobs is highly inefficient. Main story quests do not just award massive chunks of EXP; they are the sole method for unlocking critical gameplay systems.

* **System Gates**: Blacksmithing, mining, vendor shops, and travel to secondary islands are all locked behind quest progression.
* **Level Gates**: Certain high-tier zones will instantly defeat you if you enter them before reaching the recommended level. Always follow the recommended level markers on your quest log.

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Community and Code Missteps">

Because Mythfall is highly anticipated, there is a lot of misinformation surrounding codes and free rewards online. Falling for fake third-party lists is a major waste of time.

### Falling for Fake Code Tables
Many clickbait sites list guessed or completely fabricated codes. Cosmos Games is highly strict with their code redemption systems. 

* **Official Sources Only**: The only places where real codes are posted are the official Cosmos Games Discord and their official X (formerly Twitter) account.
* **Case Sensitivity**: Mythfall's redemption system is incredibly strict regarding capitalization and spacing. Copying codes directly from official developer posts is the safest way to avoid errors.
* **Pre-Release Claims**: Be wary of lists claiming to have active codes during early testing phases unless they are explicitly confirmed by developer announcements.

<Callout type="warning" title="Account Safety">
