<Section icon={Target} title="Core Controls and Movement Mechanics">

Survival in Mythfall depends heavily on your ability to navigate the environment and react to enemy patterns. The game uses a "True Action" combat system, meaning hitboxes are precise, and timing your defensive moves is just as important as your offensive output.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="PC Controls">
    - **WASD**: Character Movement
    - **Space**: Jump / Double Jump
    - **Shift**: Sprint
    - **Q**: Dash / Dodge
    - **F**: Block / Parry
    - **LMB**: Basic Attack
    - **R/E/Z/X**: Weapon Skills
  </Card>
  <Card title="Mobile Controls">
    - **Joystick**: Movement
    - **Action Buttons**: Attack and Skills
    - **On-Screen Icons**: Dodge and Block
    - **Camera**: Drag anywhere to rotate
    - **Tap Target**: Lock-on (if enabled)
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

Mastering the dash and block mechanics is the first hurdle for any beginner. Dodging provides a brief window of invulnerability, while a perfectly timed block can stagger enemies, opening them up for a counter-attack.

| Action | Stamina Cost | Effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Sprinting** | Low (Continuous) | Increases travel speed across islands |
| **Dashing** | Medium | Quick repositioning and I-frames |
| **Blocking** | Low (Per Hit) | Reduces incoming damage significantly |
| **Power Attack**| High | Breaks enemy guard and deals heavy damage |

<Callout type="warning" title="Important">
Do not deplete your stamina bar entirely. If you run out of stamina while blocking, your guard will break, leaving you stunned and vulnerable to critical hits.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="First Hour Progression Route">

The most efficient way to start your adventure is to follow the structured quest line provided by the game. Wandering aimlessly into high-level islands will often result in quick defeats. Follow these steps to establish a strong foundation for your character.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Character Creation and Race Selection">
    Choose from one of the five unique races. Each race offers different aesthetic and minor stat variations that can influence your early-game comfort. Select a race that aligns with your intended playstyle (e.g., higher mana for magic users or more health for melee builds).
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="The Tutorial Island">
    Complete every NPC request on the starting island. These quests introduce you to the basic combat loop, including how to equip weapons and use your first magic ability.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Unlocking the First Weapon">
    You will have access to 9 weapon types. Test the starter variants of the Sword, Greatsword, and Spear to see which reach and speed feel most natural to you.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Following the Story Quests">
    Story-driven quests provide the highest Experience (XP) and Gold rewards. They also guide you to the appropriate islands for your current level, preventing you from getting lost in the open world.
  </Step>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Combat Builds: Weapons and Magic Mastery">

Mythfall offers deep customization through its weapon and magic systems. You are not locked into a single class; instead, your "build" is defined by the gear you equip and the mastery points you invest.

### Choosing Your Starter Build

For your first character, it is recommended to focus on a "Balanced" build. This allows you to survive mistakes while learning boss mechanics.

### Weapon Mastery
As you use a specific weapon type, you gain Mastery XP. Higher mastery levels unlock powerful Weapon Skills that can turn the tide of a battle.

| Weapon Type | Playstyle | Key Feature |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Greatsword** | Heavy Hitter | Wide swings, high stagger |
| **Katana** | Speed/Precision | Fast combos, bleed effects |
| **Bow** | Ranged Support | Safe distance, status arrows |
| **Staff** | Magic Catalyst | Elemental spells, long range |
| **Daggers** | Assassin | Rapid attacks, high crit rate |

<Callout type="success" title="Build Synergy">
Try to match your magic element with your weapon's strengths. For example, using Ice magic to slow enemies makes it much easier to land heavy Greatsword attacks.

### Boss Mechanics
Most bosses in Mythfall follow a "Phase" system. When their health drops below 50%, they will often gain new, more aggressive attacks. Pay close attention to visual cues, such as a boss glowing red or charging energy, as these indicate a high-damage "Ultimate" move is coming.

| Boss Mechanic | How to Counter |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Red Circle (AOE)** | Dash out of the marked area immediately |
| **Yellow Flash (Parry)** | Press Block (F) just as the hit lands |
| **Blue Aura (Shield)** | Use heavy attacks to break the guard |
| **Summoning Minions** | Clear small enemies first to avoid being swarmed |

<Callout type="warning" title="Combat Warning">
Never stand still. Bosses in Mythfall are designed to punish stationary players. Always be moving, even when your skills are on cooldown.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Leveling, Farming, and Economy">

Once you have mastered the basics, your focus will shift to optimizing your character for the endgame. This involves efficient leveling routes and gathering resources for crafting.

### Resource Farming
The economy in Mythfall revolves around Mining, Blacksmithing, and Trading. You will need raw ores to upgrade your weapons, which can be found in caves spread across the various islands.

| Material | Location | Usage |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Iron Ore** | Starting Islands | Basic weapon upgrades |
| **Mythril** | Volcanic Island | Mid-tier gear crafting |
| **Star Fragment** | Sky Islands | Legendary weapon ascension |
| **Essence** | Dungeon Bosses | Magic ability enhancements |

### Trading and Blacksmithing
Do not sell your old gear immediately. You can often dismantle it at a Blacksmith to recover a portion of the materials used. Additionally, the trading system allows you to swap rare drops with other players to complete your build faster.

<Callout type="tip" title="Farming Tip">
Focus on "Farming Routes" that loop through multiple resource nodes and low-level mob camps. This maximizes both your material gain and XP per hour.
</Callout>
