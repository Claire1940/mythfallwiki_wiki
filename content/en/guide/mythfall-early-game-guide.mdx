<Section icon={Zap} title="Mastering the Controls and Movement">

Combat in Mythfall is not just about clicking; it requires precise timing and movement. The game features a robust action system where blocking an incoming attack or dodging a heavy strike can mean the difference between victory and a respawn screen.

| Action | PC Keybind | Mobile/Console | Purpose |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Basic Attack** | Left Click | Tap Screen / R2 | Primary damage source |
| **Block** | F | UI Button / L1 | Reduces incoming damage |
| **Dodge/Dash** | Q | UI Button / Circle | Avoids attacks and repositioning |
| **Sprint** | Left Shift | Joystick Push / L3 | Faster traversal across islands |
| **Skills** | 1, 2, 3, 4 | Skill Icons / Face Buttons | Powerful weapon and magic abilities |

Movement is equally important for exploration. Mythfall features verticality, meaning you will often need to climb or use abilities to reach hidden secrets and NPC quest givers located on high cliffs or floating structures.

<Callout type="warning" title="Stamina Management">
Dodging and sprinting consume stamina. In the early game, your stamina pool is limited. Avoid spamming the dodge button during combat, or you may find yourself unable to move when a boss unleashes its ultimate attack.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Target} title="The First Hour: Step-by-Step Progression">

The first hour of Mythfall is critical for setting up your character for long-term success. Instead of wandering aimlessly, follow this structured route to maximize your experience gains and unlock essential gear.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initial Questing">
    Start by speaking to the NPC at the spawn point. These story-driven quests serve as a tutorial, introducing you to the basic combat mechanics and the island's layout.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Unlock Your First Magic">
    Follow the main questline until you reach the Magic Instructor. Here, you can select your first elemental mastery. Choose one that complements your weapon choice.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Island Exploration">
    Move to the second island as soon as the questline allows. Higher-level islands offer better resource drops for mining and blacksmithing, which are essential for gear upgrades.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Join a Party">
    By level 5, you should look for other players to form a party. Grouping up allows you to tackle elite enemies and the first dungeon encounter with much less risk.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Following the story quests is the most efficient way to level up. While grinding mobs is possible, quests provide significant experience boosts and often reward you with unique equipment that cannot be found elsewhere in the early game.

| Level Range | Recommended Area | Key Objectives |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1 - 5** | Starter Island | Tutorial quests, Basic gear, First Magic |
| **5 - 10** | Whispering Woods | Mining resources, First Elite mob, Blacksmithing |
| **10 - 15** | Azure Coast | Dungeon entry, Weapon Mastery Level 5, Trading |

<Callout type="info" title="Quest Tracking">
Always keep an eye on your quest log. Some side quests offer permanent stat boosts or rare crafting materials that are vital for mid-game progression.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Weapon Types and Magic Mastery">

Mythfall features nine distinct weapon types, each with its own move set and skill tree. Your mastery with a weapon increases as you use it, unlocking more powerful skills and passive bonuses.

| Weapon Type | Playstyle | Best For |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Katana** | Fast, combo-heavy | PvP and single-target DPS |
| **Greatsword** | Slow, heavy hits | Crowd control and clearing mobs |
| **Bow** | Ranged, tactical | Safe kiting and boss support |
| **Staff** | Magic-focused | Elemental damage and healing |
| **Spear** | Reach, thrusting | Keeping enemies at a distance |

Magic mastery works alongside your weapon. You can equip different spells that use your mana pool. Combining the status effects of your magic (like freezing an enemy with Ice magic) with the high damage of a Greatsword is a common strategy for solo players.

<Callout type="tip" title="Mastery Focus">
Don't spread your mastery points too thin. It is better to have one weapon at Mastery Level 10 than five weapons at Mastery Level 2. High-level mastery unlocks "Ultimate" skills that can clear entire rooms of enemies.
</Callout>

</Section>
