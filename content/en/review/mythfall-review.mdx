<Section icon={Target} title="Progression Guide: From Novice to Legend">

Starting your journey in Mythfall can be daunting given the scale of the world. The progression is structured around a central story questline that guides you from the starter islands to the dangerous outer reaches of the map. Leveling is not just about increasing a number; it involves gathering materials for blacksmithing, discovering hidden runes to enhance magic, and completing trials that prove your worth to the various factions.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Character Creation & Race Selection">
    Choose from one of the five unique races. Each race offers distinct passive bonuses that can influence your early-game survivability or late-game damage output.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="The Tutorial Island">
    Complete the basic combat training. Focus on mastering the timing of the **block** mechanic, as it is essential for surviving the first elite enemies you encounter.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Story Questing">
    Follow the main narrative path. These quests provide the most significant experience point gains and unlock essential traversal tools like boats or gliders.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Weapon & Magic Specialization">
    Once you reach the first major hub, commit to one of the nine weapon types. Spreading your mastery points too thin early on will make mid-game bosses significantly harder.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Dungeon Exploration">
    Form a party and enter your first dungeon. This is where you will find the rare materials needed for high-tier blacksmithing and gear upgrades.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Blacksmithing Tip">

<Section icon={Shield} title="PvE Challenges: Bosses and Dungeons">

The endgame of Mythfall is defined by its challenging PvE encounters. Dungeons are not just corridors filled with enemies; they are complex environments with puzzles and environmental hazards. Bosses feature multiple phases, requiring players to adapt their strategies mid-fight. A boss might start as a slow, heavy-hitter but transition into a high-speed aerial threat once its health drops below 50%.

Teaming up with friends is highly encouraged. The game features a party system that allows for specialized roles. While there are no hard "classes" like in traditional MMOs, players naturally fall into roles based on their weapon and magic choices. A player with a shield and defensive magic acts as the vanguard, while magic users provide long-range artillery support.

<Callout type="info" title="Party Synergy">
Combining different magic types can trigger elemental reactions. For example, using a freeze spell on a target already soaked by a water-based ability will significantly increase the duration of the stun effect.
</Callout>
